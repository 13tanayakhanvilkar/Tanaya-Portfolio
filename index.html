Frequently Asked Questions

1. What makes you different from a traditional digital marketer?

I think about how marketing ideas translate into real digital experiences. Because I understand the basics of front-end development, I can spot gaps early and create solutions that are practical, scalable, and easier to implement.

2. Are you a digital marketer or a front-end developer?

I work as a digital marketer, focusing on strategy, growth, and performance. At the same time, I genuinely enjoy front-end development and often build interfaces, landing pages, and personal projects. Having experience in both helps me approach digital work more thoughtfully.

3. How does your front-end knowledge help your marketing work?

Knowing front-end development helps me think beyond ideas and campaigns. I understand how pages are actually built, which makes it easier to design experiences that load well, feel intuitive, and convert better. It also helps me collaborate smoothly with developers.

4. Do you take on development projects as well?

Yes, selectively. I usually take on front-end projects that connect closely with marketing goals, such as landing pages, campaign sites, or portfolio-style websites. My focus stays on clean UI, responsiveness, and user experience.

5. What kind of clients do you work with?

I work with brands, startups, and individuals who want clarity and purpose in their digital presence. I enjoy projects where strategy, design, and execution come together naturally.

6. Will you handle both marketing and website changes yourself?

In many cases, yes. I like being involved end to end, from planning to execution and optimization. For larger or more complex builds, I collaborate with other developers while staying closely involved in the strategy and UX direction.

7. Are you more focused on creativity or data?

I see them as equally important. I rely on data to guide decisions, but creativity is what makes the work engaging and memorable. The best results usually come from balancing both.

8. Are you open to learning new tools or technologies?

Definitely. I enjoy learning and experimenting with new tools and technologies. Staying curious helps me keep my work relevant and improve with every project.
